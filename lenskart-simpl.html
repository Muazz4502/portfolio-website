The Problem

5% payment share. Why so low?

Simpl's payment share on Lenskart stood at just 5% — meaning only 1 in 20 successful Lenskart transactions happened through Simpl. The goal was clear: increase that share. But the path wasn't.

Payment share isn't just about having a button on the checkout page. It's the result of a whole funnel — from a user not knowing Simpl exists on Lenskart, all the way to them actually completing the transaction. Every drop-off point is a lever.

The Framework

Not all users are the same

Before recommending anything, I segmented the user base into four cohorts. The right intervention depends on who you're targeting — the same nudge won't work for someone who's never heard of Simpl versus someone who uses it weekly but has never paid on Lenskart.

Cohort A
Simpl + Lenskart users who've transacted with Simpl on Lenskart
Already converted. Focus on retention and repeat purchase nudges.
Cohort B
Simpl users who are not Lenskart users
New to Lenskart. Use Simpl's own app to bring them in — scratchcards, brand promotions.
Cohort C
Both Simpl and Lenskart users — but haven't used Simpl on Lenskart
Highest-potential cohort. They have both apps but something's blocking them at checkout.
Cohort D
Lenskart users who don't have Simpl
Top-of-funnel acquisition play. Needs the most spend to convert.
👁️
Awareness
Increase top-of-funnel visibility

The first lever is simply: do people know Simpl is available on Lenskart? For Cohorts C and D, this is the primary blocker.

  • Payment page placement: Simpl currently sits below UPI apps and cards. The goal is to get into the "Recommended" section. This requires improving success rate and wallet share — metrics Lenskart's algorithm uses to rank payment options.
  • Widgets and ad cards: Beyond the payments page, Simpl can place banners and contextual widgets across Lenskart's other app surfaces — product pages, cart, homepage — to build brand recall before the user even gets to checkout.
  • Post-transaction scratchcards on Simpl (for Cohort B): When Simpl users complete any payment on the app, show them a Lenskart scratchcard. Lenskart already pays GPay and CRED for similar post-transaction screens. This creates a barter lever — Simpl promotes Lenskart, Lenskart gives Simpl better checkout placement.
🧠
Consideration
Make Simpl the obvious choice at checkout

Awareness gets users to notice Simpl. Consideration converts that notice into intent. These levers are more targeted — each mapped to specific cohorts.

  • Gamified cashback — Cohorts C & D: Pay with Simpl, spin a wheel, and get a chance to have your product for free. Gamification creates disproportionate perceived value without a disproportionate cost.
  • Extended settlement — Cohort B: Pay in 20 days instead of 15 if you buy through Lenskart. A structural benefit that rewards loyalty without cash outflows. Requires guardrails: once per user per settlement cycle.
  • Lenskart subscription free — Cohort B: A co-branded offer where using Simpl unlocks Lenskart's premium membership. High perceived value, leverages the partnership commercially.
  • Direct cashback — Cohort D only, last resort: Expensive and conditions the user to wait for offers. Use only if other levers fail and the economics make sense for Cohort D acquisition.
💳
Payment
Reduce drop-off from intent to completion

Even when a user selects Simpl, the transaction can fail — through eligibility checks, OTP drops, or payment gateway failures. Each is an opportunity.

Eligibility
  • Flexible limits: Tested this personally — a ₹3,050 cart with a ₹3,000 Simpl limit doesn't offer Simpl as an option. A 10% flexibility buffer on the limit (risk-team permitting) could recover a meaningful volume of near-limit transactions at Lenskart's higher AOV.
  • Flexible risk checks for Cohorts B & C: Users already on both platforms represent lower acquisition risk. The risk model should reflect that with slightly more permissive parameters for this segment.
Payment Completion

Current flow for non-Simpl-app users (Cohort C):

Select Simpl
Juspay PG
OTP via SMS
Enter OTP
Done

Proposed flow for Cohort C (app on phone):

Select Simpl
Redirect to Simpl app
intent (Android)
Swipe to pay
Done ↩ Lenskart
Why this matters

Removing the OTP step eliminates the most common failure point — SMS delivery issues. The app-to-app redirect is a flow users already trust from UPI, and it gives Simpl a branded touchpoint mid-transaction. On iOS, a push notification replaces the intent redirect due to platform restrictions.

  • OTP via WhatsApp or email: SMS delivery fails in low-network zones. At FamPay, adding VMN coverage improved OTP success by ~5%. WhatsApp or email OTP as a fallback directly addresses this for Simpl too.
  • Multiple payment gateway providers: All observed transactions routed through Juspay. Adding a PG orchestrator with fallback providers guards against downtime and optimises for success rate dynamically.
🔁
Post-Payment
Retain and grow

A user who transacted once is the cheapest user to convert again. Post-payment is where retention compounds into share.

  • Resurrect: Timely nudges to users who've used Simpl on Lenskart but haven't returned in a while. Targeted by purchase cycle data — glasses typically replace every 1–2 years.
  • Refer: Satisfied users are the best acquisition channel. A referral mechanic tied to Simpl or Lenskart benefits can bring Cohort B users into the funnel at lower CAC.
