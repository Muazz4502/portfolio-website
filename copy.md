# Portfolio Website — All Copy with Context

---

> **How to read this doc**
> Each piece of copy is followed by a *Why* note explaining the intent behind it — the job it's doing, the tone it's striking, or the impression it's meant to leave.

---

## NAV

- Work
- Projects
- Side Quests
- About
- Resume
- **Let's Talk**

*Why:* Nav labels are kept minimal and conversational. "Work" instead of "Experience" is less formal. "Side Quests" signals personality before someone even scrolls. "Let's Talk" as the CTA is warmer and lower-stakes than "Contact" or "Hire Me" — it invites a conversation, not a transaction.

## HERO

**Greeting:** Hey, I am Muazz

*Why:* "Hey, I am" is deliberately casual — it sounds like how a real person introduces themselves at a networking event, not a LinkedIn banner. Sets the tone for the whole page: confident but approachable.

---

**Headline:** I build products that move the needle.

*Why:* Short, active, outcome-focused. "Move the needle" anchors you as someone who cares about impact, not just output. The word "build" is intentional — PMs who say they *build* products signal ownership, not just coordination. The italic emphasis on "move" adds rhythm and draws the eye.

---

**Tagline:** Product Manager @ FamPay · Payments and Fintech · Bangalore

*Why:* This is the credentialing line — it answers "who are you exactly" in one breath. Payments + Fintech signals domain depth. Bangalore signals you're in the ecosystem. The dots (·) break it into scannable chunks without feeling like a resume line.

---

**CTAs:** View Resume · LinkedIn →

*Why:* Two CTAs, not one — gives the visitor a choice based on intent. "View Resume" is for recruiters who want to move fast. "LinkedIn" is for people who want to learn more before committing. The arrow on LinkedIn implies it opens externally, setting expectations.

*Why:* Metrics are the proof layer. The arrows (↑↓) make direction scannable at a glance. "2 core features launched" grounds the impact in concrete deliverables, not just optimisation.

*Why:* Each bullet follows the same structure: action → outcome. "Rebuilt", "Led", "A/B tested" are strong verbs that show ownership. Specific details like "48h to 6h", "NPCI", "sub-₹200", and "double digits" signal technical fluency and real involvement — they're details only someone who actually did the work would know.
