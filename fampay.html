The Company

India's first neobank for teenagers

FamApp (by Trio) is building the financial infrastructure for a generation that's been overlooked by every bank in the country. We give teenagers a spending account, UPI, and a numberless FamCard, the kind of product that didn't exist when most of us were 14.

The mission is to build a generation that is financially aware, smart, and independent. FamApp does this through frictionless payments, parental controls, and financial literacy features embedded directly into the product.

How I Worked

A few things I actually believe

Payments is brutal to get wrong. A failed transaction is real money, real friction, and a real reason to leave. Most of how I worked came from taking that seriously.

01
Measure it before you touch it
I never started a project without first understanding the baseline. In payments you really cannot afford to find out things are broken from a support ticket.
02
Get into the technical detail
I spent real time learning NPCI flows, banking partner constraints, and where UPI actually fails. You make better calls when you understand what you're building on top of.
03
Don't wait for things to be clean
Monitoring and features ran at the same time. Waiting for infrastructure to be "ready" before shipping product is how you fall behind. You figure it out in parallel.
04
Test things you think you already know
The confirmation screen experiments moved double digits because nobody had actually tested that surface before. Strong intuitions make good hypotheses. They don't replace experiments.
