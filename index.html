Side Quests

Learning in the wild

Startup School April 2026 · Bangalore

YC Startup School India

Y Combinator's first physical Startup School in India — 2,000 founders in one room, six speakers who actually built it, and the recurring feeling that the best advice doesn't change.

Takeaways

  • Customer obsession isn't a value on a slide. It's a daily practice — every founder on stage kept coming back to it.
  • Lived experience with the problem is the real moat. Trust is the one thing AI won't replace for a while.
  • Almost every speaker quoted a Paul Graham essay. Different essays. Same instinct.
  • Charge early, bet early — attack the ceiling, not the floor.
FinTech Conference March 2026 · Bangalore

Razorpay FTX'26

India's most exclusive FinTech conference — founders and operators pressure-testing AI, payments infrastructure, and what the next layer of financial tech actually looks like.

Takeaways

  • Post-transaction screens should explain why a transaction happened, not just confirm that it did — context beats confirmation.
  • AI optimises execution, not product-market fit. The core thesis still has to come from you.
  • Authentication is the biggest blocker for agentic payments. Trust is the unlock nobody has cracked yet.
  • We're 8–10 months behind on AI-native products. Still catchable — but the window is closing.

About

The short version

My goal is simple — walk into a coffee shop, look around, and quietly count at least 3 people using a product I built, without them ever knowing it was me.

I'm Muazz — PM at FamPay, where I own payments health for India's first teen neobank. Before this, I ran growth experiments at Avaana Capital.

The 9-to-6 is half the story. I'm building an XR infrastructure startup and exploring what a unified gig worker platform could look like for India.

I believe a startup doesn't need a perfect product at launch — it needs an insanely great experience, starting with how attentive you are to every early user.
