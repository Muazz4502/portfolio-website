# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Mixed audience — recruiters screening for PM roles, fellow PMs evaluating depth and craft, startup founders scouting collaborators, and early-stage investors looking for builder evidence. Each skims differently: recruiters want proof of outcomes, founders want evidence of taste and judgment, peers want intellectual honesty about process. The site needs to work for all four in a single scroll.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio for Muazz, PM at FamPay working on payments infrastructure and fintech. The portfolio is the product — it needs to demonstrate not just what was built, but how Muazz thinks, what he values, and that he has the personality worth working with. Success means someone finishes the page and wants to reach out, not because the resume was strong but because the person came through.

## Brand Personality

Modern, smart, personality-driven. Not corporate. Not a template. Confident without being loud. Editorial without being pretentious. The PM who has strong opinions and can also back them with numbers.

## Anti-references

Generic PM portfolio templates. Side-stripe border callouts. Identical card grids. "Building products that move the needle" level copy (technically on the site — needs refinement). Anything that looks like it could be swapped with any other PM's portfolio by changing the name.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the person, not the role.** Credentials are table stakes. What you're building on weekends, how you think out loud, what you geek out on — that's what differentiates.
2. **Outcomes are the format.** Numbers, changes in behavior, things that shipped. Qualitative storytelling earns the right to exist only if the outcome is already visible.
3. **Every section earns its presence.** If a section can't answer "why would a founder care about this?" it needs to work harder or disappear.
4. **Personality is not decoration.** The tetris loader, the iPhone mockup, the side quests — these are features, not flourishes. Design should reinforce them, not compete with them.
5. **Trust the reader.** Don't explain what you're about to say. Don't restate section labels in body copy. The audience is smart.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA minimum. Reduced motion support for the scroll animations and loader. Color contrast for the accent on cream background should be verified (red on cream can fail at small sizes).
